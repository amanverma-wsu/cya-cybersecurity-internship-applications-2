# Reflection Report

## Activity Description

For this Career Development Experience, I researched and applied to 98 cybersecurity internships for Summer 2026. I targeted roles spanning security engineering, application security, DevSecOps, SOC analysis, and general cybersecurity at companies including Amazon, PayPal, Thermo Fisher Scientific, Capital One, and CISA. My approach was to apply broadly across sub-domains while tailoring my resume to emphasize the most relevant experience for each category of role, my bug bounty background for AppSec roles, my CI/CD projects for DevOps positions, and my NCL competition results for SOC and analyst roles.

## Technical and Professional Decisions

The most significant decision I made was **choosing breadth over depth in my applications**. As a junior still exploring which area of cybersecurity fits me best, I intentionally applied across multiple sub-domains rather than narrowing to one niche. This let me compare how different companies frame similar roles and helped me understand which job descriptions genuinely excited me versus which ones I was applying to out of obligation.

I also decided to **lead my resume with projects and independent research rather than coursework**. My bug bounty recognitions from Ferrari, BASF, Philips, and the United Nations carry more weight with hiring managers than listing classes, and my BugHunter framework and CVE scanner demonstrate the kind of hands-on work that security teams actually value. This was a deliberate choice to present myself as a practitioner, not just a student.

## Contributions

This was an independent effort. I conducted all research, wrote all application materials, and submitted each application myself. I used Jobright AI as a discovery and tracking platform to manage the volume of applications, and I leveraged LinkedIn and company career pages for direct applications. I also received feedback on my resume from peers and mentors at WSU, which helped me tighten my bullet points and improve how I quantified project impact.

## Quality Assessment

I am partially satisfied with my performance. On the positive side, I committed serious effort — 98 applications is substantial, and I learned a lot about how cybersecurity hiring works, what companies look for, and how to position my experience. However, if I were to repeat this process, I would **start earlier**. Many of the strongest positions (Amazon, PayPal) opened in late summer and closed by the time I applied in September. Starting the search in July or August would have given me a better shot at top-tier programs. I would also invest more time in **networking with current and former interns** at target companies before applying, since referrals significantly improve response rates.

## Career Alignment

This experience directly advances my goal of securing a cybersecurity internship before senior year. Beyond the applications themselves, the process forced me to articulate my strengths, identify which sub-domains of cybersecurity excite me most, and recognize gaps I can fill through coursework and certifications. I came away with a clearer focus on **application security and DevSecOps** as the areas where my skills and interests converge, which will guide my elective choices and personal projects going forward.
